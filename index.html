From Discovery to Story — 3 Sentences👤
5 min · opens Thursday PM
Before you touch any slides, each person writes these three sentences individually. Then read them to each other. They are the skeleton of your validation slide.
We thought…
We found…
So we changed…
These three sentences = your validation slide. Copy them directly into Beat 04 (Validation) of the storyboard below.
01
Storyboard Before Slides👥
15 min
Map each pitch beat — one key message per beat. Agree on the flow before anyone opens a slide tool. The slide is the last thing you make, not the first.
BEAT 0160s
Hook — Human Story
Open with one specific person and one specific moment. Name them. Make the panel feel the problem before you name the solution. No company name yet.
BEAT 0260s
Problem — Scale & Cost
Who has it, how often, how costly. One number that makes the size undeniable. Show the math, don't quote a report.
BEAT 0390s
Solution — Show, Don't Describe
Show the prototype here. One sentence on what it does, then show it live or on screen. Let the product speak. Don't narrate every click.
BEAT 0460s
Validation — What You Learned
We thought… We found… So we changed… Intellectual honesty wins the room. The panel has seen too many teams that never tested anything.
BEAT 0560s
Market — Bottom-Up Math
TAM / SAM / SOM with real numbers. Show the calculation, don't cite a market report. Your entry point matters more than the total market.
BEAT 0660s
Business Model
Who pays, how much, when. One clean sentence. Then one slide on unit economics if you have them. No hedging, no "it depends."
BEAT 0745s
Unfair Advantage
What makes you structurally hard to copy? If you don't have one yet, say so honestly — and say what you'd need to build it. That's more credible than inventing one.
BEAT 0830s
Team
Why you for this specific problem — not general praise. One sentence per person, specific to the challenge.
BEAT 0930s
Next 90 Days
Three concrete milestones. Not "grow users" — specific, dated, measurable. Shows the panel you know what comes after Friday.
BEAT 1030s
Ask & Close
Specific ask + one memorable closing line. End with a statement, not "any questions?" The last sentence should be impossible to forget.
